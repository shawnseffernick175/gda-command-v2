# Doctrine → Doors Mapping (Envision-Operated, Team-Aware — 13 Doors)

Each of the 13 rebuild doors must enforce at least one Doctrine principle. This is how the tool stops being a CRM and starts being doctrine made executable.

**Ownership:** The tool is Envision's workspace. All doors default to Envision scope. Partner Intel (door 12) is the cross-OU teaming radar — that's where Teamwork doctrine lives operationally.

## How Teamwork (Principle 3) lives in the tool

AJ's doctrine demands cross-OU collaboration. Since Envision is the only OU operating the tool right now, Teamwork shows up as:

1. **Partner Intel door (12)** — Envision sees what Riverstone and PD Systems are pursuing so they don't step on each other and they catch teaming moments.
2. **Teaming flags on Opportunities (1) + Pipeline (2)** — when an opp needs HUBZone, V3 Veteran, or IC clearance, the tool surfaces the partner who unlocks it.
3. **Capture teaming worksheet (3)** — partner certs/vehicles/PP pulled directly into the proposal.
4. **Cross-OU action items (10)** — "ask Angela," "ping Gina," tracked with due dates.
5. **GDA narrative in Company Profile (7)** — when Envision positions upmarket, the 3-pillar Enable/Protect/Train story is available.

If AJ later approves enterprise rollout, doors 1-11 already carry OU tags — Riverstone and PD Systems get logins, the data model federates, no rebuild.

## What the doctrine forbids the tool from doing

- ❌ Hide expired certs from proposal generation (Ethics)
- ❌ Auto-promote opportunities to pipeline (Market/Mission/Brand Focus — user qualifies)
- ❌ Render cartoon charts (Data First — ECharts only)
- ❌ Allow committee-owned action items (Relentless Execution — individual owner required)
- ❌ Bury sources or remove citation trail (Data First)
- ❌ Auto-subscribe to financial sources (Ethics — manual upload preserves audit)
- ❌ Claim Riverstone or PD Systems certs on Envision-only proposals (Ethics)
- ❌ Pull Riverstone or PD Systems financials into Envision rollups (Ethics — they're partners, not consolidated subsidiaries inside this tool)
